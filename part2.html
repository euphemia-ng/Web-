Cognac-France

As a famous brandy of the long-standing wine-making country - France, Cognac is known as the type of wine that has been associated with the earthly sacrifices of kings since ancient feudal times. .....

With an important influence from hundreds of years ago, to this day, Cognac has become one of the famous and extremely popular wines around the world.

Usually, each Cognac is carefully crafted and stored as the grape harvest season begins and ends. Producers usually store the wine in a deep cellar dug underground to prevent air from changing the color and flavor of the wine, keeping the cognac from becoming sour. The process of storing Cognac takes place for a very long time, up to 1/3 of human life to ensure absolute flavor and color of the wine.

Sake-Japan

Known as the old traditional Japanese liquor, Sake is a colorless alcohol like Gin and Vodka. .....

Sake also has a light yellow color, transparent alcohol and an average alcohol content of 15% (quite high compared to other famous wines in the world when their average alcohol content is only about 12%).

Sake is fermented during the aging process by the action of yeast, after fermentation, the sugar in the cereal will also be converted into alcohol. In general, the process of making Sake is similar to the process of making beer. The reason Sake makers don't use common edible rice to make sake is because Sake rice requires starch to be in the center of the grain. Therefore, the selected rice grain must be very white, pure and not as clear as the usual rice.
